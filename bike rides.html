BIKE RIDE

Join small guided group of bikers, enjoy a challenging tamhini ghat ride.

Overview

  • Tamhini Ghat, also known as Tamhini Ghats, is a mountain passage located in the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra, India
  • It connects the Mulshi and Tamhini regions and is renowned for its scenic beauty, lush green landscapes, and cascading waterfalls.
  • It's a favorite destination for bikers and nature enthusiasts looking for a refreshing ride away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Route and Distance

  • Starting Points: Pune
  • Distance: The distance is approximately 70-80 km.
  • Route from Pune: Pune -> Mulshi -> Tamhini Ghat -> Kolad

Highlights of the Ride

  • Scenic Views: The ride offers stunning views of the Western Ghats, with dense forests, rolling hills, and numerous waterfalls, especially during the monsoon season.
  • Mulshi Lake: A serene spot near the starting point of Tamhini Ghat, ideal for a quick stop to enjoy the tranquility of the lake.
  • Waterfalls: During the monsoon, the ghat is dotted with numerous waterfalls, making for picturesque stops along the way.
  • Biodiversity: The region is rich in flora and fauna, offering a chance to spot various species of birds and wildlife.
  • Twisty Roads: The winding roads and hairpin bends provide an exhilarating riding experience for bikers.

POINTS OF INTEREST

  • Mulshi Lake and Dam: A serene spot to relax and enjoy the view before entering Tamhini Ghat.
  • Waterfalls: Numerous waterfalls like the Devkund Waterfall and others that appear during the monsoon season.
  • Kundalika River: A popular spot for white-water rafting, located near the end of Tamhini Ghat towards Kolad.
  • Temples and Local Villages: Explore local temples and experience the rural life of the villages in the region.

Safety Tips

  • Ride Cautiously: The ghat section has narrow and winding roads with sharp bends. Ride carefully, especially during the monsoon when the roads can be slippery.
  • Check Your Bike: Ensure your bike is in good condition, with brakes, tires, and lights all functioning properly.
  • Protective Gear: Always wear a helmet, gloves, and appropriate riding gear to ensure safety.
  • Stay Hydrated: Carry enough water and stay hydrated throughout the ride.
