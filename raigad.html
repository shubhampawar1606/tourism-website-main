RAIGAD TREK

Raigad trek is without a doubt, one of the most renowned travel destinations in the Maharashtra.

Overview

  • Raigad Fort, located in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India, is a historically significant site and a popular trekking destination.
  • It was the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and is renowned for its majestic views, rich history, and challenging trek.

Historical Significance

  • Capital of the Maratha Empire: Raigad Fort served as the capital of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj’s kingdom in 1674.
  • Architectural Marvel: The fort complex includes several important structures like the King’s palace, Queen’s quarters, public durbar, watchtowers, and the Samadhi (memorial) of Shivaji Maharaj.
  • Strategic Location: The fort is situated at an altitude of 820 meters (2,700 feet) above sea level and is strategically positioned, making it a formidable fortress in its time.

Trekking Route

  • Base Village: The trek starts from the base village called Pachad.
  • Duration: The trek typically takes around 2-3 hours to reach the top, depending on the fitness level of the trekkers.
  • Difficulty Level: Moderate. The trail includes steep steps, rocky paths, and narrow passages.
  • Steps: There are approximately 1,737 steps leading up to the fort.
  • Ropeway: For those who prefer an easier route, a ropeway service is available that takes visitors from the base to the top of the fort.

POINTS OF INTEREST

  • Maha Darwaja: The main entrance gate of the fort, known for its impressive architecture.
  • Holicha Mal: A large open ground where festivals and public gatherings were held.
  • Hirkani Buruj: A watchtower with a thrilling legend of a brave woman, Hirkani, who climbed down the fort at night to reach her child.
  • Jagadishwar Temple: A temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, where Shivaji Maharaj worshipped.
  • Takmak Tok: A cliff edge offering panoramic views of the surrounding Sahyadri mountain range and the Konkan region.
  • Samadhi of Shivaji Maharaj: The final resting place of the great Maratha king.

TIPS FOR TREKKERS

  • Carry Adequate Water: The trek can be physically demanding, so it’s essential to stay hydrated.
  • Wear Comfortable Clothing and Footwear: Opt for trekking shoes with good grip and comfortable, breathable clothing.
  • Pack Light: Carry only the essentials like water, snacks, a first aid kit, and a raincoat during the monsoon.
  • Start Early: Begin the trek early in the morning to avoid the midday heat and to have ample time to explore the fort.
